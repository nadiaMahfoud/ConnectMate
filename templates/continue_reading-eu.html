Europe's most famous places

Paris, France

What more can be written about Paris that hasn’t been written a thousand times. It’s a beautiful city, of course. But then again, there’s the Paris beyond the obvious sights like the Eiffel Tower and joining long queues at the Louvre. For interesting urban walking, take a wander through The Petite Ceinture Railway, an abandoned railway line that predates the Paris Métro where colourful flowers peak through the old tracks and street art adorns old platforms. You could easily spend a weekend in Paris just gallery hopping, but one of the more unusual spaces is 59 Rivoli, an infamous former squat turned legit studios with exhibits across 6 floors. It’s worth visiting the outside if you’re pushed for time, the facade is like an installation in itself. The gardens of Paris are rightly celebrated, but few people know about the Jardin Francs-Bourgeois-Rosiers, a secret garden tucked behind a 17th-century mansion in the Marais district. Paris comes in at 27 in our best places in Europe to visit guide.

Barcelona, Spain

Nestled along the northeastern coast of the Iberian Peninsula, Barcelona is a vibrant and enchanting city that seamlessly blends its rich history with a modern, cosmopolitan atmosphere. Famous for its iconic architecture, including Antoni Gaudí's Sagrada Familia and Park Güell, Barcelona offers visitors a sensory feast of art, culture, and cuisine. Explore the narrow streets of the Gothic Quarter, relax on the picturesque beaches, and savor tapas in lively local markets. Whether you're captivated by the architectural wonders or charmed by the lively street life, Barcelona promises an unforgettable experience.

Rome, Italy

As the eternal city, Rome is a living testament to centuries of history, art, and civilization. The Colosseum, Roman Forum, and Pantheon stand as awe-inspiring reminders of the city's ancient past. Stroll through the charming cobblestone streets, toss a coin into the Trevi Fountain, and savor authentic Italian cuisine in Trastevere. Rome's rich cultural heritage, combined with its warm and welcoming atmosphere, makes it an enduring favorite among travelers seeking a perfect blend of history, romance, and culinary delights.

Prague, Czech Republic

Known as the "City of a Hundred Spires," Prague captivates visitors with its fairy-tale charm and rich architectural heritage. The Old Town Square, Prague Castle, and Charles Bridge showcase the city's medieval and Gothic splendor. Wander through the winding streets, visit historic landmarks, and enjoy panoramic views from the Prague Castle. With its Old World charm, vibrant arts scene, and welcoming locals, Prague is a destination that transports visitors back in time while offering a modern and dynamic cultural experience.

Athens, Greece

Athens, the cradle of Western civilization, invites travelers to immerse themselves in ancient history and mythology. The Acropolis, Parthenon, and Temple of Olympian Zeus stand as enduring symbols of Greece's classical past. Beyond the historical sites, Athens offers a lively atmosphere, with bustling markets, vibrant neighborhoods like Plaka, and a burgeoning culinary scene. Visitors can enjoy breathtaking views of the city from Lycabettus Hill or relax in one of the many charming cafes that dot the city streets.

Dubrovnik, Croatia

Dubrovnik, often referred to as the "Pearl of the Adriatic," is a stunning coastal city steeped in history. Encircled by medieval walls, the Old Town of Dubrovnik transports visitors to a bygone era with its well-preserved architecture and charming streets. Walk along the city walls for panoramic views of the Adriatic Sea, visit the iconic Rector's Palace, and soak in the unique ambiance of Stradun, the main thoroughfare. Dubrovnik's combination of historical allure and coastal beauty makes it a captivating destination for travelers seeking a perfect blend of culture and seaside relaxation.
